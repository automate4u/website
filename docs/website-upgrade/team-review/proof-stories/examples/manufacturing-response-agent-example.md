## Industry And Business Type

Industrial manufacturing / parts distribution. B2B. SMB or mid-market.

## Before State

Customers asked about availability, lead times, quote status, order timing, substitutions, and next steps through email, phone, and rep-specific threads.

Staff manually searched inventory, ERP/order records, quote trackers, customer history, and internal notes before responding.

Sales lost time to repeated status checks instead of higher-value sales conversations. Operations was interrupted when sales needed stock, timeline, or fulfillment context.

## Channels Involved

- Phone calls for urgent availability and order questions.
- Email for quote and order follow-up.
- Contact forms for quote intake.
- CRM for customer and follow-up history.
- ERP/order management for status and inventory context.
- Spreadsheets or quote trackers where the official system did not cover the full workflow.
- Product notes or approved knowledge base.

## Systems And Tools Involved

Use safe public categories:

- CRM
- ERP or order management system
- Inventory source
- Quote tracker
- Shared inbox
- Approved knowledge base

Generalize specific ERP, customer account, product category, and part-number details.

## What The Automation Did

- Classified the request type: availability, quote, order status, substitution, timeline, or general support.
- Extracted part number, account context, urgency, and missing details.
- Checked approved source context where available.
- Drafted a response or prepared internal notes for review.
- Created or updated CRM activity.
- Created a sales, parts, or operations task.
- Escalated uncertainty or commitment-heavy cases.

After classification, the workflow routed the request to the right owner with a structured summary and next-step recommendation.

## Human Controls

Human-owned:

- Pricing exceptions.
- Final quote approval.
- Substitutions.
- Delivery promises.
- Account-sensitive decisions.
- Unusual customer requests.

Escalate:

- Conflicting inventory data.
- Missing part numbers.
- Special pricing.
- Customer relationship concerns.
- Uncertain lead times.

The AI should not independently commit price, inventory, delivery date, or custom quote terms.

## Outcome And Value

Safe outcome language:

- Faster request triage.
- Cleaner handoffs between sales and operations.
- Less manual copy/paste from emails into CRM or task queues.
- Better visibility into quote and order follow-up.

Metrics to collect:

- First response time.
- Quote cycle time.
- Requests routed without manual triage.
- CRM follow-up completion.
- Staff time spent on repetitive availability/status questions.

## Buyer Fear This Helps Address

- Spend anxiety: starts with a bounded response workflow.
- AI mistakes: pricing and commitments stay under staff approval.
- Integration complexity: starts with approved sources and task creation.
- Customer experience risk: improves response speed without uncontrolled promises.
