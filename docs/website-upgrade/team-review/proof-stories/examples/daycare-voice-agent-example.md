Private childcare operator.

## Industry And Business Type

Daycare / childcare / early education. B2C. Often multi-location.

## Before State

Front desk teams handled calls during busy classroom transitions, parent drop-off, pickup windows, and staff coordination periods.

Common calls included enrollment questions, tour requests, absence notices, pickup notices, program availability, pricing questions, and policy questions.

Staff often had to check calendars, classroom capacity, enrollment details, attendance tools, internal messaging, and policy documents while staying available to children, families, and teachers.

## What The Automation Did

- Answered routine calls with a calm, parent-friendly tone.
- Captured caller name, child/student context, location, request type, and urgency.
- Identified enrollment, tour, absence, pickup notice, program availability, or policy questions.
- Provided approved routine answers where safe.
- Routed tour/enrollment inquiries to the right follow-up path.
- Notified staff or classroom group for routine absence or pickup notices.
- Escalated sensitive or unclear situations to authorized staff.
- Logged the call summary and next step.

## Human Controls

Human-owned:

- Pickup authorization.
- Custody-sensitive matters.
- Health, medication, or allergy concerns.
- Identity verification.
- Enrollment decisions.
- Pricing exceptions.
- Uncertain policy answers.

The AI should not independently approve high-risk pickup/custody changes or make safety decisions.

## Outcome And Value

Safe outcome language:

- Fewer routine front-desk interruptions.
- More consistent handling of common parent questions.
- Cleaner call summaries and staff handoffs.
- Sensitive situations flagged instead of handled blindly.

Metrics to collect:

- Routine calls handled.
- Missed calls reduced.
- Staff interruptions reduced.
- Tour requests captured.
- Absence/pickup notices logged.
- Sensitive escalations documented.

- Sensitive workflow risk: high-risk cases stay with authorized staff.
