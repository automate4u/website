## Internal Reference

Internal project nickname: Appointment intake and booking follow-up

Based on: appointment-based professional services such as clinics, wellness practices, dental, orthopedic, osteopathy, massage, consulting, or advisory teams.

Do not reveal: client name, practitioner names, patient/client details, medical or personal details, exact booking software, intake documents, pricing.

## Safe Public Label

Appointment-based professional services practice.

## Industry And Business Type

Professional services / healthcare-adjacent / appointment-based services. Mostly B2C, sometimes B2B. SMB.

## Before State

Staff manually handled inquiries, appointment questions, intake forms, document follow-up, reminders, cancellations, and routing to the right practitioner or staff member.

New inquiries could stall if a form was incomplete, a staff member needed to call back, or the correct next step was unclear.

The team needed better consistency without weakening privacy, care, or professional judgment.

## Channels Involved

- Phone calls.
- Email.
- Contact forms.
- SMS reminders.
- CRM or client records.
- Calendar or booking software.
- Intake forms.
- Approved service descriptions and appointment policies.

## Systems And Tools Involved

Use safe public categories:

- Booking or calendar tool.
- CRM or client records.
- Intake forms.
- Email/SMS reminders.
- Document collection.
- Staff task queue.

Generalize practice management software, practitioner names, intake forms, and client records.

## What The Automation Did

- Captured service need, contact details, urgency, and availability.
- Routed inquiries by service type or staff owner.
- Prepared booking or callback tasks.
- Sent approved reminders or document requests.
- Summarized context for staff review.
- Escalated sensitive, regulated, emotional, or unclear requests.

## Human Controls

Human-owned:

- Professional advice.
- Clinical, legal, or financial judgment.
- Eligibility decisions.
- Sensitive questions.
- Pricing exceptions.
- Complaints.
- Anything requiring empathy or nuance.

The AI should not diagnose, advise, make regulated decisions, or replace practitioner judgment.

## Outcome And Value

Safe outcome language:

- More consistent intake.
- Fewer manual reminders.
- Cleaner staff handoffs.
- Faster path from inquiry to booked or assigned next step.

Metrics to collect:

- Intake completion rate.
- Time to booked next step.
- No-show or reminder completion.
- Manual follow-up reduced.
- Staff interruptions reduced.

## Buyer Fear This Helps Address

- AI mistakes: professional judgment stays human.
- Sensitive workflow risk: escalation rules and audit trail.
- Staff replacement concerns: AI handles intake support, not expertise.
- Customer experience risk: faster response without careless advice.
