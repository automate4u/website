## Before State

Customer questions arrived through website chat, email, social messages, and order support forms.

Staff repeatedly answered product questions, order status questions, return questions, sizing or compatibility questions, and follow-up requests.

Support and marketing were disconnected, so repeated customer questions did not reliably become FAQs, content ideas, email flows, or product-page improvements.

## What The Automation Did

- Classified messages by product question, order status, return, shipping, sizing, compatibility, complaint, or sales opportunity.
- Used approved product and policy context to answer routine questions or draft replies.
- Routed uncertain or high-value cases to staff.
- Created follow-up tasks for sales or support.
- Suggested FAQ/content updates from repeated questions.
- Connected support patterns to marketing follow-up opportunities.

## Human Controls

Human-owned:

- Refund exceptions.
- Product guarantees.
- Complaint handling.
- Public claims.
- High-value customers.
- Brand-sensitive responses.
- Final publishing of content or campaigns.

The AI should not invent product claims, approve refunds outside policy, or publish customer-facing marketing without review.

## Outcome And Value

Safe outcome language:

- Faster triage of repeated support questions.
- More consistent answers from approved policy/product context.
- Cleaner handoff for exceptions.
- Better visibility into repeated customer questions.
- Stronger connection between support insights and marketing content.

Metrics to collect:

- Support messages classified.
- Routine replies drafted.
- Response time.
- Tickets routed to the right owner.
- FAQ/content improvements identified.
- Follow-up campaigns or tasks created.

## Buyer Fear This Helps Address

- Customer experience risk: routine answers use approved context.
- Brand risk: claims and campaigns stay reviewed.
- Spend anxiety: starts with support triage before deeper automation.
- Integration complexity: connects support, ecommerce, and marketing in stages.
