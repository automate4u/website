# Safe Claims And Metrics Library

Use this when turning proof notes into website copy. The goal is to sound specific and credible without overclaiming.

## Strong But Safe Claim Patterns

- Designed to reduce routine manual triage.
- Helps staff respond faster to repeated requests.
- Creates a cleaner handoff between AI and staff.
- Turns calls, emails, chats, and forms into structured workflow actions.
- Keeps sensitive decisions under human approval.
- Gives managers better visibility into response time, escalations, and follow-up.
- Reduces copy/paste between disconnected systems.
- Helps teams recover missed opportunities without promising uncontrolled automation.
- Starts with a bounded workflow before expanding into broader operations.
- Improves consistency across locations, teams, or channels.

## Metrics We Can Use When Verified

- Calls answered.
- Missed calls recovered.
- Requests handled.
- First response time.
- Quote turnaround time.
- Appointments booked.
- Follow-ups completed.
- Manual steps removed.
- Admin hours saved.
- Staff interruptions reduced.
- Tickets triaged.
- Emails classified.
- Draft responses prepared.
- Human escalations logged.
- Cost per request.
- Handled minutes.
- Workflow completion rate.
- Tool-call success rate.
- Failed or duplicate actions reduced.
- Unsupported answers reduced.
- Retrieval answer quality improved.
- Mean time to resolution.
- Deployment confidence.
- Regression rate.
- Sync failure rate.
- Retry success rate.

## Good Range Language

Use ranges when exact metrics would be identifying or are not approved.

- "reduced response time from days to same-day"
- "saved an estimated 10-20 admin hours per week"
- "handled hundreds of routine requests per month"
- "removed several manual handoffs from the intake workflow"
- "helped staff respond within business hours instead of waiting for manual review"
- "created a clearer escalation path for sensitive requests"
- "improved grounded answer quality in offline evaluations"
- "reduced unsupported responses by using approved source material and fallback rules"
- "reduced failed or duplicate system actions through validation, retries, and idempotency"
- "improved incident response with structured logs, dashboards, alerts, and runbooks"

Only use these patterns after the team confirms they are directionally true.

## Qualitative Outcome Language

Use these when metrics are not available yet:

- "The team gained a clearer review queue."
- "Requests became easier to route and follow up."
- "Staff no longer had to start every response from a blank inbox thread."
- "Leadership could see which requests were being handled, escalated, or delayed."
- "The workflow made the handoff more consistent without removing human judgment."

## Risky Language To Avoid

Do not use:

- "Guaranteed ROI."
- "Fully replaces your staff."
- "Eliminates the need for admin."
- "Never makes mistakes."
- "Autonomous decision-making for sensitive cases."
- "Instant transformation."
- "Set it and forget it."
- "Uses your private data to train AI."
- "Fully compliant" unless legal review confirms the exact context.

## Safer Alternatives

Instead of "replace staff":

- "reduce repetitive admin load"
- "free staff from routine interruptions"
- "give staff more time for higher-value work"

Instead of "fully automated":

- "automated where safe, escalated where needed"
- "approval-based workflow"
- "human-controlled automation"

Instead of "AI knows the answer":

- "uses approved business context"
- "checks configured sources"
- "routes uncertain cases to staff"

Instead of "cheap AI":

- "priced around real usage, monitoring, integrations, and support"
- "starts with a bounded pilot"
- "ties spend to workflow volume and measurable value"
